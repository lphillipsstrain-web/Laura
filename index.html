About

I move through the world as a learner, a maker, a systems thinker, and a modern heathen. My practice threads together clay, steel, code, story, and ritual—but also light, color, texture and form.

For years, my work was rooted in listening closely, translating complexity, setting clear expectations, and building quiet structures so other people could move more easily through muddled systems. That instinct hasn’t changed, but the tools have.

These days I’m exploring how digital landscapes echo the physical ones I love. I’m learning how risk, infrastructure, and human behavior weave together, not as a hard career pivot but as another thread in the larger loom. At the same time, I’m deepening my spiritual path, walking the Way of the Wyrd. Paying attention to the subtle patterns that surface across both art and tech.

I’m also an autistic woman rebuilding a life after burnout. This site is a place to hold all of that: the mythic and the mundane, the tactile and the digital. The work of surviving and the work of making something beautiful.
