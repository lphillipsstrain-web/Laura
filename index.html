About

I move through the world as a learner, an artist, a systems thinker, and a modern heathen. My practice threads together code, clay, pigment, and light. Variable mediums all give me different ways to feel the world and make sense of it.

I’ve always held a steady commitment to sustainability—environmental, social, and economic. It shapes how I think about materials, communities, and the systems around us. That perspective continues to guide my choices, in both art and work: use what’s already here, move carefully, and look for approaches that help people and places endure.

My professional life has long centered on understanding complexity and making it navigable for others. My work was rooted in listening closely, setting clear expectations, and building resilient structures so other people could move more easily through muddled systems. That instinct hasn’t changed, but the tools have.

Lately, I’ve been drawn toward questions of digital safety, infrastructure, and how people behave inside the systems they rely on. I’m exploring how digital landscapes echo the physical ones I love. I'm studying how people, systems, and unseen forces influence one another. It’s a quiet thread in my life, not the whole tapestry.

I’m also an autistic woman rebuilding a life after burnout. This site holds all the threads I’m weaving back together: the everyday and the esoteric, the tactile and the digital, the quiet work of healing and the joyful work of creating something beautiful.

Current threads
  • Documenting wild moments where the world and my pulse finally align.
  • Relying on ritual and meaning-making to guide me through rebuilding.
  • Learning how to move through life with more gentleness toward my own wiring.
  • Seeking work that honors curiosity, pattern-finding, and quiet impact.
Tools & Terrains
  • Liminal zones where boundaries soften and information shifts its form.
  • Bearing witness to capture the quiet ruptures and revelations that unfold at the edges of things.
  • System traces—timeouts, stalls, recoveries—that mirror the cadence of my own overwhelm and repair.
  • Attunement practices for sensing how disparate influences converge, stabilize, and reveal a way forward.
